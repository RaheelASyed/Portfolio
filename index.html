CliniVision - Project Code

At UVA, I joined a club called Project Code, where students can propose project ideas, and other students can join them based on their interests. I joined a Project called CliniVision, where the goal is to create a website that can analyze X-ray images and use AI to identify fractures and other issues. I became the front-end team lead and taught my team everything I learned about Web Design. We worked together to make wireframes for each page and create a beautiful high-fidelity website prototype. Unfortunately, the student running the team became occupied with other commitments, so the team was disbanded. However, I plan to join another project in the future and complete it. My time as the front-end team lead was unforgettable. No one on my team has prior experience in Web Design, but they were all willing to learn. Everyone had different majors and backgrounds, including one member who was a master's student. Leading and teaching my diverse team helped me become better at clarifying concepts by explaining them based on what they know and understand.

2023 - present

UX/UI Designer - Weblems

Weblems is a startup company, a collaborative effort between my brother, two other UVA students, and myself. As a UX/UI Designer, I play a crucial role in assisting the CTO with creating Figma designs for clients and publishing them using WordPress. Designer and I assist the CTO with making Figma designs for clients and publishing them through WordPress. An important lesson the CTO taught me is not to "reinvent the wheel." He showed me where I could see professional website designs on Envato Elements and Dribbble and told me that instead of designing from scratch, I could use their designs as inspiration and tweak them as needed. 2022 - 2023

UX/UI Designer - College Marketplace

My Brother introduced me to his friend who works in full-stack development and told him that I was trying to get more into the UX/UI Design field. He told me he needed assistance on a website he was creating called College Marketplace, where college students could buy and sell things to each other. I enthusiastically accepted his offer and began assisting him by advising him on his design and creating different iterations for pages. I also taught him things I learned from online courses, such as making variables for colors in Figma and using auto layouts. One specific feedback I recall giving is when he first showed me his home page, which was full of carousels for each category. I told him that the design was too cluttered and repetitive. We worked together to devise a solution where there is only one carousel for choosing the category of items, and items would be displayed under it. This experience helped me improve at giving helpful, constructive feedback and taught me how to iterate on a design to improve it.

2022 - 2023

UX/UI Designer - PolyUno

I joined PolyUno as an intern after gaining an interest in web design. PolyUno is a SaaS company, so I thought it was perfect to explore the field more. The CEO provided me with free online courses that helped me learn Figma, HTML, and CSS. A noteworthy experience at the company was when the CEO wanted to test what I learned. He informed me about a new website they were creating called WeMeetUp that would allow people to share their availability during the week so that they could schedule times to meet. He wanted me to create a design for the website that his designers could use as a basis to create the actual website. I worked diligently to ensure the color palette, typography, and spacing made sense and fit with accessibility standards. I also user-tested with my parents and siblings to certify that the navigation was straightforward. After completing the Figma design, the CEO and his Designer viewed it and were very impressed since it was my first time designing an entire website. Additionally, the designers added many constructive comments on my design to help me improve. This experience greatly boosted my confidence in UX/UI design and encouraged me to continue exploring the field.
